Travel Information

Visitors to Glacier National Park should plan ahead, particularly during the peak season from late June through early September. Vehicle reservations are required for certain high-traffic areas, including Going-to-the-Sun Road. Reservations can be made in advance through Recreation.gov, and it is recommended to book as early as possible, as entry passes and lodging often sell out quickly. Campgrounds, backcountry permits, and guided tours should also be reserved in advance to secure availability. When planning your visit, consider seasonal weather conditions and park accessibility. Snow can linger at higher elevations into early summer, and sudden weather changes are common. Visitors should bring layered clothing, adequate water, bear spray for hiking in backcountry areas, and reliable maps or GPS tools. Cell service is limited throughout much of the park. Arriving early in the day can help avoid congestion, and using shuttle services where available can reduce parking challenges. Proper preparation ensures both a safe and enjoyable experience in this expansive wilderness.
